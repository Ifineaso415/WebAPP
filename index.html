What is Pickleball?

Pickleball is a fun, fast-growing paddle sport that combines elements of tennis, badminton, and ping-pong. Played on a small court with a perforated plastic ball and paddles, it's easy to learn, social, and accessible for all ages and skill levels. Whether you're looking for friendly rallies or competitive play, pickleball is a great way to stay active and connect with the community. Curious about how the game is played? Check out the court layout below to see how the unique dimensions and zones make pickleball so engaging for everyone!

  • • Games are played to 11, win by 2.
  • • Serve underhand and diagonally.
  • • Only the serving team can score points.
  • • No volleys in the "kitchen" (non-volley zone).

Tip: You don’t need fancy gear—just grab a paddle and join the fun!

Pickleball Court Layout

  • Court size: 20' x 44' (same for singles and doubles)
  • Net height: 36" at sidelines, 34" at center
  • Non-volley zone ("kitchen"): 7' from net on each side
  • Service areas: Divided into left and right by centerline
